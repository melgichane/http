Sex Trafficking

Sex trafficking is slavery that uses different forms of manipulation to force and exploit a victim into acts of sexual conduct. Anyone can be trafficked, no matter age, sex, gender, or race. 82% of reported human trafficking incidents in the United States between January 2008 and June 2010 involved sex trafficking.

Labor Trafficking

The physically exploitation of labour occurs in numerous industries globally. In the United States, common types of labor trafficking include domestic servants, farm workers, and factory workers. Labor trafficking accounted for 11% of incidents, and other or unknown forms of human trafficking collected to make up the remaining 7%.
